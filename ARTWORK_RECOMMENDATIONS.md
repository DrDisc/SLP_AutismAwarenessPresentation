# Artwork Recommendations for SLP Autism Awareness Handouts

This document provides guidance on finding complementary artwork for your presentation handouts. All sources listed are free and appropriately licensed for use.

## Recommended Artwork by Handout

### Handout 1: What is a Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP)?

**Suggested Images:**
- Professional therapist/clinician with child in clinical/educational setting
- Diverse children communicating in various ways
- Brain/neuroscience imagery (representing speech-language development)
- Children with speech bubbles or communication symbols
- Multi-disciplinary team illustration
- Inclusive/diverse families

**Recommended Sources:**
- **Unsplash** (www.unsplash.com) - Free professional photography
  - Search: "speech therapy", "child communication", "diverse children", "professional consultation"
- **Pexels** (www.pexels.com) - High-quality free images
  - Search: "therapy", "children learning", "communication"
- **Pixabay** (www.pixabay.com) - Large collection of free images
  - Search: "speech pathology", "children talking", "professionals working with kids"

**Specific Image Suggestions:**
- Portrait of diverse children of various ages
- Parent-child interaction in therapeutic setting
- Communication/speech bubble graphics
- Professional woman/man in clinical setting with child

## Download Instructions

### For Unsplash (www.unsplash.com):
1. Search for relevant terms (e.g., "speech therapy", "parent child playing")
2. Click on image
3. Click "Download Free" button
4. Save to `/media/` folder
5. Rename with descriptive name (e.g., `slp_therapy_session.jpg`)

### For Pexels (www.pexels.com):
1. Search for terms
2. Click on image
3. Click "Download" button
4. Save to `/media/` folder

### For Pixabay (www.pixabay.com):
1. Search for terms
2. Click on image
3. Click "Download" button (choose resolution)
4. Save to `/media/` folder

## Important Considerations

### Copyright & Licensing
- All sources recommended here offer **free licenses** for use
- No attribution required (though appreciated) for most images
- Always verify the specific license for each image
- These sources are safe for educational and commercial use

### Image Selection Tips
- Choose images showing **diversity** (race, ability, family structure)
- Select images with **positive, supportive** tones
- Prefer **authentic photos** over generic stock images when possible
- Ensure images show **actual interaction** (not posed/artificial)
- Look for images representing **real families and professionals**

### Accessibility
- Ensure good **contrast** for readability
- Use images that don't rely solely on color
- Include **alt-text** descriptions for all images
- Resize appropriately for handout format
