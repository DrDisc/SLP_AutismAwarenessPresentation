## ğŸ“ Common Quick Questions

**Q: How many handouts should I print?**  
A: For a booth, start with 100-150 total. For mailing, 20-50 to targeted families. See PRINTING_GUIDE.md for details.

**Q: Should I use the images?**  
A: Optional! Handouts work great with or without. Images add visual interest.

**Q: Can I change the content?**  
A: Absolutely. Adapt examples, add local resources, personalize stories. Make it yours!

**Q: What if I'm nervous?**  
A: Normal! You're an expert. You know this content better than anyone. Just speak naturally.

**Q: How long should it take?**  
A: Presentation: 5-7 minutes. Booth conversations: 5-20 minutes each. Allow 2 hours total if running a booth.

**Q: What technology do I need?**  
A: For presentation: projector/screen and laptop. For booth: just printed materials (tablet optional for AAC demo).

**Q: Who should I give handouts to?**  
A: Everyone interested! Parents, teachers, childcare providers, other professionals.

**Q: Can I digitally deliver this?**  
A: Yes! You can email handouts, share them online, or present virtually. Works either way.

**Q: How do I follow up?**  
A: Use the Follow_Up_Letter.md template. Send within 48 hours. Offer a free consultation call.

## ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips

### Before Presenting
- Practice with a colleague for feedback
- Time yourself to hit 5-7 minutes
- Personalize examples with your real experience
- Gather stories from your practice (keep confidential)

### During Presenting
- Speak clearly and slowly
- Make genuine eye contact
- Show enthusiasm for the topic
- Allow pauses for questions
- Be warm and approachable

### At the Booth
- Stand (don't sit), be inviting
- Listen more than you talk
- Take notes on what families ask
- Use their name if they introduce themselves
- Give them something to take home

### Following Up
- Send emails within 48 hours
- Personalize with specific things you discussed
- Offer a free 15-minute consultation call
- Make it easy to book with you
- Follow up again if they don't respond
