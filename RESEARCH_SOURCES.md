# Research Sources for SLP Autism Awareness Presentation

## Credible Sources Consulted

### 1. American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA)
**Source:** ASHA Practice Portal - Autism and Autism Spectrum Disorder  
**URL:** https://www.asha.org/practice-portal/clinical-topics/autism/  
**Relevance:** Professional standards, SLP roles, evidence-based practices

**Key Information:**
- SLPs play a central role in screening, assessment, diagnosis, and treatment of persons with ASD
- Early intervention may improve long-term outcomes for many children
- Core features of ASD: social communication, language/cognitive skills, behaviors/emotions
- Benefits of early diagnosis include access to appropriate services and early intervention

### 2. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
**Source:** CDC Autism and Developmental Disabilities Monitoring (ADDM) Network  
**URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/autism/  
**Relevance:** Prevalence data, early signs, screening recommendations

**Key Statistics:**
- 1 in 36 children identified with ASD (2020 data)
- Boys 4x more likely to be diagnosed than girls
- Most families report concerns by 18 months
- Early intervention services can begin before formal diagnosis

### 3. Autism Speaks
**Source:** Autism Speaks - Speech Therapy  
**URL:** https://www.autismspeaks.org/speech-therapy  
**Relevance:** Family-friendly explanation of speech therapy benefits

**Key Points:**
- Speech therapy addresses both verbal and non-verbal communication
- Helps with social communication skills
- Can include augmentative and alternative communication (AAC)
- Family involvement is crucial

### 4. Interagency Autism Coordinating Committee (IACC)
**Source:** 2021-2023 IACC Strategic Plan for Autism Research, Services, and Policy  
**URL:** https://iacc.hhs.gov/publications/strategic-plan/2023/  
**Relevance:** Federal policy, research priorities, lifespan services

**Key Findings:**
- Timely diagnosis allows for timely supports and services
- Early intervention facilitates positive outcomes
- Need for improved diagnostic tools across diverse populations
- Importance of neurodiversity-affirming care

### 5. College of Audiologists and Speech-Language Pathologists of Ontario (CASLPO)
**Source:** CASLPO Professional Standards  
**URL:** https://www.caslpo.com  
**Relevance:** Ontario-specific education and licensing requirements

**Ontario SLP Requirements:**
- Master's degree in Speech-Language Pathology (2-3 years)
- Undergraduate degree (typically in related field)
- Registration with CASLPO
- Ongoing professional development requirements

### 6. Additional Academic Sources

**Source:** National Research Council (2001). *Educating Children with Autism*  
**Key Point:** Participation of families in services can help reduce stress

**Source:** Dawson et al. (2010). Early Start Denver Model  
**Published:** *Pediatrics*, 125(1)  
**Key Finding:** Randomized controlled trial showing benefits of early intervention

**Source:** Watson et al. (2013). Communicative gesture use in infants

**Key Finding:** Children with autism use fewer gestures as infants/toddlers

## Early Intervention Research Summary

### Critical Period
- First 2-3 years of life are crucial for brain development
- Neural plasticity is highest in early childhood
- Early signs can be detected as early as 6-18 months

### Benefits of Early Intervention
1. Improved communication skills
2. Enhanced social interaction
3. Reduced problematic behaviors
4. Better long-term outcomes
5. Family stress reduction
6. Increased independence

### Evidence-Based Approaches
- Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Interventions (NDBI)
- Family-centered practice
- Play-based interventions
- Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC)
- Social communication interventions

## Ontario-Specific Information

### Education Path to Become an SLP in Ontario
1. **Undergraduate Degree** (4 years)
   - Bachelor's degree (often in linguistics, psychology, or related field)
   - Prerequisite courses in speech, language, hearing sciences

2. **Master's Degree** (2-3 years)
   - Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Speech-Language Pathology
   - Clinical placements and supervised practice
   - Research thesis or project

3. **Registration** 
   - Register with CASLPO (College of Audiologists and Speech-Language Pathologists of Ontario)
   - Pass certification examinations
   - Maintain continuing education requirements

### Where SLPs Work in Ontario
- Hospitals and rehabilitation centers
- Schools and early intervention programs
- Private practice
- Community health centers
- Developmental services
