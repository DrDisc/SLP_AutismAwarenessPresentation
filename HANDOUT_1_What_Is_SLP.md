# What is a Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP)?

## Your Partner in Communication Development

---

### Who Are We?

Speech-Language Pathologists (SLPs) are healthcare professionals who specialize in:
- **Communication** (speaking, understanding, and social interaction)
- **Language development** (words, sentences, and conversation)
- **Feeding and swallowing**
- **Cognitive-communication** (thinking, memory, problem-solving)

### How SLPs Help Children with Autism

**Communication Development:**
- Building vocabulary and language skills
- Improving understanding of language
- Developing conversational abilities
- Teaching alternative communication methods when needed

**Social Communication:**
- Understanding and using social cues
- Taking turns in conversation
- Making and keeping friends
- Understanding emotions and perspectives

**Alternative & Augmentative Communication (AAC):**
- Picture communication systems
- Sign language
- Communication apps and devices
- High-tech and low-tech solutions

**Parent & Caregiver Coaching:**
- Strategies to use during daily routines
- How to encourage communication at home
- Understanding your child's communication style
- Building on your child's strengths

### When Should You See an SLP?

**Consider an SLP evaluation if your child:**

**By 12 Months:**
- Doesn't babble or make sounds
- Doesn't use gestures (waving, pointing)
- Doesn't respond to their name

**By 18 Months:**
- Isn't saying any words
- Doesn't point to show you things
- Has lost previously acquired skills

**By 24 Months:**
- Isn't combining two words
- Doesn't copy words and actions
- Doesn't follow simple directions

**At Any Age:**
- You have concerns about communication
- Others have difficulty understanding your child
- Your child seems frustrated when trying to communicate
- Limited eye contact or social interaction
- Difficulty with eating or swallowing

**Remember:** You know your child best. If you have concerns, seek evaluation. Early is better!

### What to Expect from SLP Services

**Assessment (1-3 sessions):**
- Observation of your child's communication
- Standardized testing (age-appropriate)
- Parent interview and questionnaires
- Play-based evaluation
- Comprehensive report with recommendations

**Treatment:**
- **Frequency:** Typically 1-2 times per week
- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes per session
- **Setting:** Clinic, home, school, or online
- **Approach:** Play-based, naturalistic, family-involved
- **Progress monitoring:** Regular updates and goal adjustments
